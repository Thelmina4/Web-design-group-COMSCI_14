History Of Gospel Music

Phillip D. Bliss

1870s - 1930s

The origins of Gospel music can be dated back to the 19th century. The phrase "Gospel music" was first introduced in 1874, by a singer and composer named Phillip D. Bliss when he published a collection of hymns. In the following 60 years, gospel music was incorporated into American churches, and many well-known gospels artists came as a result of this such as George F Root.

1960s - 1980s

By the 1960s, gospel music had become a popular and recognised genre of music and many gospel artists began to enter mainstream secular music, such as Aretha Franklin. In the following two decades artists like Kirk Franklin and Yolanda Adams would go between gospel and secular music, becoming very successful artists.

Aretha Franklin, one of the most successful gospel singers ever.

Present Day

Since the 1990s, gospel music has changed and now there are many different types of gospel music, such as urban contemporary gospel music and country gospel music. Millions of gospel records are sold yearly and gospel music, as a genre, continues to grow in popularity with many additional artists embracing the genre.
